A minimalist graphic poster design in the tradition of Hara Kenya's Muji manifesto campaigns, Kazumasa Nagai's symbolic poster work, and Josef Müller-Brockmann's Swiss International Typographic Style. This is NOT a photograph — it is a graphic design composition created by a senior Japanese art director for an editorial magazine poster insert.

Composition: 4:5 vertical format, 1080x1350. A single-color field background in warm off-white cream with the barest hint of paper texture (subtle fibers visible on close inspection, like hand-made washi paper). Absolute cleanliness. No photographic elements.

Sole graphic element (center-left of frame): A single minimalist organic abstract shape, hand-drawn in soft dusty sage green #7a9b7e, resembling a gentle brushstroke, a stylized single leaf, or an abstract silhouette of a sprouting form — flowing and calming, not rigid. The shape occupies roughly the center-left third of the frame, placed with generous negative space around it. The brushstroke has authentic hand-made imperfections, subtle texture variation, and slight opacity falloff at the edges like real sumi-e ink work but in sage green pigment. Size: approximately 35 percent of the frame height.

Layout logic (editorial poster grid):
- Left-aligned typography dominant
- Brushstroke element placed on the vertical center-left axis, above the headline
- Strong negative space breathes around everything

Style palette: Cream off-white (#f5f1e8), dusty sage green (#7a9b7e), soft charcoal (#2a2a2a), warm gray (#666666). That is the ENTIRE palette. Nothing else.

Texture: Subtle authentic washi paper fiber texture across the background — visible only on close inspection, completely silent at scroll distance.

Strict rules:
- This is a GRAPHIC DESIGN POSTER, not a photograph — zero photographic elements, zero 3D rendering, zero realistic imagery.
- The single abstract shape must look AUTHENTICALLY hand-drawn with subtle imperfections, NOT computer-generated geometric.
- All Japanese glyphs must render with absolute accuracy and clean editorial typesetting.
- Generous breathing negative space — minimum 40 percent of the frame must be clean background.
- No additional graphics, icons, logos, arrows, sparkles, stars, or badges beyond the brushstroke, the text, and the CTA pill.
- Absolutely no currency symbols.
- The word "勧誘" appears ONLY in the subcopy list with a clear negating context ("声かけ・勧誘不要") — and must render cleanly.
- No MLM visual cues whatsoever.

Final goal: A poster that looks like it could hang in a gallery or an architect's office, but functions as a Meta Feed ad. Stops the scroll through graphic sophistication and negative space — the exact opposite of the typical loud feed ad.
